Install Order

  1. Disconnect the negative battery terminal before starting.
  2. Remove the glove box or lower dash panel to access the HVAC housing (passenger side).
  3. Unplug the resistor and motor connectors, inspecting both for heat damage.
  4. Remove the motor's retaining screws and lower it out of the housing.
  5. Swap in the new motor, then the new resistor, and reconnect (with fresh pigtail if needed).
  6. Pack connectors with dielectric grease before final seating.
  7. Reconnect the battery and test all blower speeds before closing up the dash.
From the bench Don't skip the current draw check on the new motor if your old resistor melted. A motor pulling too many amps will cook a brand-new resistor just as fast as the old one, and you'll be back here in a month.
